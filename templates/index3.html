Diet Plan for Diabetic Patients


Early Morning

Early Morning A diabetic patient should start the day by drinking lukewarm water. This will help to flush out the toxins from the body. You can also add one teaspoon of methi seed powder. This will eventually help in managing the blood sugar levels.


Breakfast

A bowl of porridge and cucumber Muesli with milk and one cucumber or tomato A bowl of wheat flakes with milk and one cucumber or tomato Prepare moong-dal chilla with at least 2-3 veggies A bowl of vegetable daliya or upma 2 rotis with 1 bowl of sabzi with a bowl of curd 2 slices of whole wheat bread and omelet with egg white and veggies


Mid-Morning

A cup of green tea with some roasted channa One whole fruit- apple/pear/orange/few cubes of papaya/guava


Lunch

Only eating to fill the stomach should not be the mantra for diabetic patients. Food loaded with nutrients should be the agenda. Here are some lunch options that diabetic patients can try for lunch- 2 chapatis with 1 bowl of sabzi+ 1 bowl of dal/sprouts/curd/buttermilk/2-3 pieces of chicken or fish 1 big bowl of vegetable daliya khichdi with curd 1 bowl of salad/ 2 cucumbers/2 tomatoes+ half a bowl of brown rice + 1 big bowl of sabzi + 1 bowl of dal/sprouts/curd/buttermilk/2-3 piece of chicken/fish


Evening Snack

1 whole fruit 1 handful boiled or roasted chana Puffed rice Buttermilk Sandwich


Dinner

1 bowl of salad/2 cucumbers/2 tomatoes + 2 chapatis with 1 big bowl of sabzi + 1 bowl of dal/sprouts/curd/buttermilk/ 2-3 pieces of chicken/fish 1 big bowl of vegetable daliya khichdi with curd


Bedtime Snack

To reiterate the same, diabetic patients should not keep long gaps between two meals. Hence, before going to bed it is recommended to eat nuts




Exercise

Engaging in regular physical activity is beneficial for overall health and can play a significant role in preventing and managing diabetes. Here are the benefits of running, walking, cycling, swimming, and yoga in relation to diabetes prevention:

Exercise 1: RUNNING

Improved Insulin Sensitivity: Regular running helps improve insulin sensitivity, allowing cells to better respond to insulin and lower blood sugar levels.

Weight Management: Running is an effective way to burn calories and maintain a healthy weight, which is crucial for diabetes.

Cardiovascular Health: Running enhances cardiovascular fitness, reducing the risk of heart diseases common complication associated with diabetes.



Exercise 2: WALKING

Accessibility: Walking is a low-impact exercise that is easy to incorporate into daily life, making it accessible for people of all fitness levels.

Blood Sugar Control: Regular walking can help control blood sugar levels and improve insulin sensitivity.

Weight Management: Like running, walking aids in weight management and can contribute to maintaining a healthy body mass index (BMI).



Exercise 3: SWIMMING

Low Impact: Swimming is a low-impact exercise, making it suitable for individuals with joint issues or mobility concerns.

Muscle Engagement: Swimming engages multiple muscle groups, contributing to improved insulin sensitivity and better blood sugar control.

Stress Reduction: The rhythmic nature of swimming can help reduce stress, which is beneficial for overall health and diabetes prevention.



Exercise 4: CYCLING

Muscle Activation: Cycling engages large muscle groups, promoting glucose uptake and utilization, which is beneficial for managing blood sugar levels.

Cardiovascular Benefits: Cycling is an aerobic exercise that improves cardiovascular health and reduces the risk of diabetes-related complications.

Exercise 4: YOGA

Stress Reduction: Yoga incorporates mindfulness and relaxation techniques that can help manage stress, which is important for preventing diabetes.

Improved Insulin Sensitivity: Certain yoga poses and practices may contribute to improved insulin sensitivity and glucose

Flexibility and Balance: Yoga improves flexibility and balance, promoting overall physical well-being and reducing the risk of injury.
